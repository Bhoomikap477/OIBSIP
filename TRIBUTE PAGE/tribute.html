Sudha Murthy

Sudha Murty, born on August 19, 1950, is an Indian educator, author, and philanthropist. Co-founder of the Infosys Foundation and wife of N. R. Narayana Murty, she is renowned for her social work, receiving the Padma Shri in 2006 and the Padma Bhushan in 2023. Murty's career spans computer science and public health initiatives with the Gates Foundation. She is recognized for founding orphanages, promoting rural development, and advocating for education in Karnataka schools. Additionally, she established the Murty Classical Library of India at Harvard University, aimed at preserving Indian literary heritage. Murty's literary contributions in Kannada and English, including novels like "Dollar Bahu" and stories like "Runa," have inspired both television series and films. Beyond her writing, she has acted in films such as "Pitruroon" and "Prarthana." Her life reflects a profound commitment to philanthropy, literature, and societal advancement, making her a beacon of inspiration for many.
